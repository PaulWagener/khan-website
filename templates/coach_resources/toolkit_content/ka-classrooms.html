KA classrooms

Khan Academy classrooms are most successful when they include flexible and engaged teachers and students.

What is the role of the teacher in a Khan Academy classroom?

In a self-paced classroom, teachers become masterminds who can interpret various streams of data and facilitate appropriate learning experiences as a result. Teachers can expect to…

  • use data to identify struggling students, diagnose why they’re struggling, and help them overcome those challenges
  • teach small groups, hold 1-on-1 sessions, and facilitate collaborative projects
  • direct students to specific videos and other resources to learn or refresh their knowledge
  • determine when and how students are ready to be assessed
What is the role of the student in a Khan Academy classroom?

In a Khan Academy classroom, students take ownership for their learning and engage deeply with the material. Students

  • take initiative when stuck by watching a video, taking hints, or asking a peer
  • set appropriate goals and work hard to achieve them
  • help peers understand concepts while respecting that everyone learns at different paces
  • challenge themselves by applying their math skills to real-world problems and projects
