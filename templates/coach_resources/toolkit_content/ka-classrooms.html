KA classrooms

Khan Academy classrooms are most successful when they include flexible and engaged teachers and students.

What is the role of the teacher in a Khan Academy classroom?

Teachers play many fundamental roles in Khan Academy classrooms, where they

  • use Khan Academy data to identify struggling students, diagnose why they’re struggling, and help them overcome those challenges
  • guide students to set appropriate goals
  • direct students to appropriate videos and other resources to learn or refresh their knowledge on various topics
  • run small group and 1-on-1 sessions to teach or reinforce concepts
  • create and administer meaningful projects to excite students about content and/or to reinforce knowledge
  • teach students how to tutor their peers
  • cultivate a positive and supportive learning environment
  • determine when students are ready for exams
  • design and administer exams
  • flexibly adapt to meet the ever-changing needs of students
  • share content and product feedback with the KA team
What is the role of the student in a Khan Academy classroom?

In a Khan Academy classroom, students take ownership for their learning and engage deeply with the material. Students

  • set appropriate goals and work hard to achieve them
  • try to help themselves when stuck by watching a video and/or taking hints
  • help their peers understand concepts and don’t just give them the answer
  • ask their peers or teacher for help if they’ve already tried to help themselves
  • continue working on exercises after they’ve earned proficiency if they think they need the extra practice
  • ask questions and continue to try to understand a concept until they’ve fully mastered it
  • work hard
  • regularly review concepts so they don’t forget them
  • try to apply their knowledge to real-world problems
  • recognize that everyone learns different concepts at different paces
  • learn at their own pace
What roles do teachers and students play in individualized classrooms?

When we talk about individualized classrooms on this site, we’re describing classrooms in which

  • students use Khan Academy to learn concepts deeply and to mastery at their own pace
  • teachers develop collaborative classroom environments where students tutor one another, solve problems in groups, and tackle hands-on projects that help them better understand their world
